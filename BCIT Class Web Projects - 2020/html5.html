My Trip to Japan

It was always a dream of mine to visit Japan, and in 2018 I made that dream a reality. I left school to take a break from studying and worked as a barista for over a year. During that time I saved up money each month until I had enough to travel to Japan for two weeks. The experience was incredible, I visited so many wonderful sites and saw so many beautiful things. Let me tell you all about it!

Osaka

The best place in Osaka was definitely the Owl Cafe! They have lots of owls that you can interact with, take pictures with and even pet them. I was fortunate to come at a slow time and had most of the cafe to myself.

This was a great city, the only thing bad was that the owls in the cafe were all tied to wooden posts.

Kyoto

The second place I was visited was Kyoto, where a store famous for dyeing their cloth with indigo was located. While I was there the owners of the store/company let me into their house/store where they sat me down and explained the history of their company. Did you know that fire-fighters back in the day used to wear indigo-dyed clothing soaked in water when going into the fire? This is because true indigo dye doesn't burn!

Kyoto was the best, in my opinion. Great food and great sights.

Tokyo

The last city I was able to visit was Tokyo, which is definitely the biggest city I have ever been to (so far). The transit system there is incredibly complicated but with enough English signs so that even I could navigate them! Only had to ask for directions once.

I wish Tokyo wasn't so expensive, a great city with great people though.
